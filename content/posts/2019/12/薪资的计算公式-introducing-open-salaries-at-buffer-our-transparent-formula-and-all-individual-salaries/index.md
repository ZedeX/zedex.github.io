When we first established the [Buffer values](https://buffer.com/about#buffer-values) that we wanted to have as the center of our company culture, we knew that sticking to these ideas would be an incredible challenge. Especially since we’ve seen before that these values can easily end up being little more than a set of words written on a piece of paper.

In our [culture deck](http://www.slideshare.net/Bufferapp/buffer-culture-04), the second value on our list at [Buffer](http://bufferapp.com/) is **“Default to Transparency.”** With this point especially, we started to think about everything we do within the company and how we could change it to something more transparent.

Sticking to radical transparency was probably both one of the most frightening and exciting things to do over the past months. It has meant to open up and make ourselves extremely vulnerable for ideas, since they were easily accessible to everyone on the team. Let me give a few examples of where we’ve started to put more transparent workflows in place:

- **Complete openness about our revenues and user numbers**: Every month [we publish the investor update](https://open.buffer.com/buffer-november-update-2347000-run-rate-1189000-users/) here on the Open blog.
- 完全公开我们的收入和用户数量

- **Progress reports** on [Buffer’s customer support](https://open.buffer.com/what-we-have-learned-about-weekends-and-how-fast-to-hire-the-november-2013-buffer-happiness-report/), [Buffer blog performance](https://open.buffer.com/end-of-year-planning-for-the-buffer-blog-and-our-most-recent-stats/), [Buffer for Business performance](https://open.buffer.com/how-much-revenue-did-buffer-for-business-generate-in-november/): Holding ourselves accountable and putting all these monthly reports out there, was another big step.
- 公开客户支持的进度报告、系统性能、业务表现

- **Every internal email sent between any 2 people on the team has a certain list cc’ed that is [accessible for everyone](https://open.buffer.com/buffer-transparent-email/):** For example, if 2 engineers email with each other, they cc the engineers list, if it’s people on our customer support team they have a support email list cc’ed. Stripe was a great inspiration for this. ( [More about this](http://joel.is/post/69066438261/how-we-handle-team-emails-at-our-startup-defaulting-to))
- 团队内的邮件互相公开

- **Personal self-improvement at Buffer**: [To make this fully transparent, we are using IDoneThis](http://idonethis.com/) to help us share our improvements daily and keep ourselves accountable. We’ve also published more around this [here](http://www.fastcompany.com/3009585/7-slightly-crazy-ways-to-build-a-happy-productive-and-transparent-company).

From the examples above, I often reflect on the power of transparency. I believe that it has such a unique potential to empower and inspire a team that it has largely transformed how we run Buffer.

One key reason transparency is a such a powerful value for a company’s culture is trust: **Transparency breeds trust, and trust is the foundation of great teamwork.**

Another thing that happens when you default to transparency is that it breaks down barriers within the team drastically. This is simply because defaulting to transparency means that you share every idea or new direction very early, before it’s completely solid.

Recently, we also made the decision to apply our ideas around transparency to compensation. We hope this might help other companies think about how to decide salaries, and will open us up to feedback from the community. So here we go, for the first time, we are making our new, internal salary formula public and including all the individual salaries too:

### What is Open Salaries?

At Buffer, we have the concept of “Open Salaries.” We have a simple formula to calculate salaries and we share this with the whole team.

### Why share what we each make?

One of the highest values we have at Buffer is transparency. We do quite a number of things internally and externally in line with this value. Transparency breeds trust, and that’s one of the key reasons for us to place such a high importance on it. Open salaries are a step towards the ultimate goal of Buffer being a completely “Open Company.”

### The salary formula 薪酬计算公式

Salary = job type X seniority X experience + location (+ $10K if salary choice)

- **job type = base 工作种类决定基本工资**
- happiness hero = $45,000
- content crafter = $50,000
- engineer = $60,000
- designer = $60,000
- Operations officer base = $70,000
- Executive officer base = $75,000

- **seniority = base multiplier 职位级别系数**
- Senior + 5% base and 3k/$m revenue
- Lead +7% base and 4k/$m revenue
- VP + 10% and 6k/$m revenue
- C-level +20% and 8k/$m revenue
- COO +20% and 10k/$m revenue
- CEO + 20% and 12k/$m revenue

- **experience = multiplier 工作年限系数**
- Master: 1.3X
- Advanced: 1.2X
- Intermediate: 1.1X
- Junior: 1X

- **location = additional 根据上班地点不同的加成参数**
- A: +$22K (e.g. San Francisco, Hong Kong, Sydney, London, Paris, New York)
- B: +$12K (e.g. Nashville, Birmingham, Vienna, Austin, Vegas, Tel Aviv)
- C: +$6K (e.g. Tallinn, Warsaw, Bucharest, Santiago)
- D: +$0K (e.g. Manila, Delhi, Hanoi)

- **equity / salary choice 个人可以选择薪资/持股的package（参考雷军小米的工资包）**
- you get a choice of more equity or more salary, if you choose salary, you get +$10K

### Buffer Bootcamp: Our 45-day freelance period 45天试用期，结束后再选择薪资/持股的具体数字

For the [45-day “bootcamp” period](https://open.buffer.com/inside-buffer-bootcamp/) for new hires, we take the +10K (salary over equity) option and then translate that into a daily rate. At the end of 45 days, the person can choose to reduce salary for more equity.

## Thoughts about the future of Buffer’s salary formula 关于此公式的未来设想

One of the most important parts of the salary formula is that this is a living document. For example, we’ve iterated to this version of the formula [from a previous version that you can find here](http://99u.com/articles/15527/the-age-of-salary-transparency). A key change we made here is to add career progression into the formula, which as Buffer has grown over the past, has become an important addition.

You can see the internal version of the formula [here](https://buffer.hackpad.com/Open-Salaries-xuJkLYmLq0x) where we’ll continually make updates to it. We expect to make frequent changes to it and also blog about them as they happen.

It feels incredibly liberating to put this out into the open. I’d love your thoughts, ideas and feedback on our formula and how we can improve it further. Please ask me any questions in the comments below.
